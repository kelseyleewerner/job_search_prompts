# Human-Sounding Writing Guidelines for LLMs

---

## Overview

This guide outlines practical techniques for making LLM-generated text sound more natural, human, and context-aware. The focus is on improving tone, structure, and clarity while avoiding common signals of machine-generated writing.

---

## Core Principles

Human-sounding writing typically feels like:

- A person speaking to a specific audience  
- With a clear purpose  
- In a tone that fits the situation  
- Using concrete, specific language  
- Without trying to sound overly perfect  

AI-generated writing often feels like:

- A system optimizing for completeness and polish  
- Generic, overly balanced, or symmetrical  
- Detached from a clear voice or intent  

---

## Best Practices

### 1. Clear Point of View

- Write with intent and judgment
- Emphasize what matters
- Maintain a consistent voice

---

### 2. Natural Phrasing

Prefer:

- Everyday words  
- Direct phrasing  
- Simple verbs  

Avoid:

- Inflated vocabulary  
- Corporate or abstract language  

**Example**

| Less Natural | More Natural |
|--------------|-------------|
| facilitate improved organizational velocity | help teams move faster |

---

### 3. Sentence Variety

Use a mix of:

- Short sentences  
- Medium-length explanations  
- Occasional longer sentences  

Avoid repetitive rhythm.

---

### 4. Avoid Over-Explaining

- Skip obvious explanations  
- Trust the reader  
- Be concise where possible  

---

### 5. Use Specific Language

Replace vague phrases like:

- "a wide range of"
- "various aspects of"

With concrete details.

---

### 6. Limit Symmetry

Avoid excessive patterns like:

- “not only X, but also Y”  
- “whether A, B, or C”  

Use sparingly.

---

### 7. Match Tone to Context

Adjust based on:

- Audience  
- Purpose  
- Medium  
- Urgency  

---

### 8. Use Contractions

Examples:

- it’s  
- don’t  
- can’t  

Helps reduce stiffness.

---

### 9. Allow Slight Imperfection

Include:

- Occasional sentence fragments  
- Conversational phrasing  
- Light informality  

Avoid sounding overly polished.

---

### 10. Avoid Fake Enthusiasm

Avoid:

- “Absolutely!”  
- “Great question!”  

Use grounded, appropriate tone instead.

---

### 11. Reduce Transition Overuse

Avoid excessive use of:

- moreover  
- furthermore  
- additionally  

Prefer simpler transitions or paragraph breaks.

---

### 12. Use Realistic Examples

**Weak Example**

> Businesses can use this in many industries.

**Stronger Example**

> A small team might use this to draft release notes, clean up emails, or summarize meetings.

---

### 13. Show Appropriate Uncertainty

Use when relevant:

- probably  
- likely  
- depends on  
- seems  

Avoid sounding equally certain about everything.

---

### 14. Remove Redundant Summaries

- Don’t restate obvious points  
- End naturally when the idea is complete  

---

### 15. Avoid Overusing Lists

- Use paragraphs when more natural  
- Lists should serve clarity, not structure for its own sake  

---

### 16. Use Strong Verbs

| Weak Phrase | Stronger Alternative |
|-------------|---------------------|
| made improvements to | improved |
| provided assistance with | helped |
| conducted an analysis of | analyzed |

---

### 17. Add Subtle Attitude

Examples:

- “That’s the real issue.”  
- “This works on paper, but breaks quickly.”  

Adds personality and authenticity.

---

### 18. Avoid Overused Phrases

Common offenders:

- it’s worth noting  
- in order to  
- delve into  
- comprehensive  
- key takeaway  

Use sparingly.

---

### 19. Read It Internally

Ask:

- Does this sound like something a real person would say?  
- Does it match the context?  

---

### 20. Prioritize Usefulness Over Completeness

Focus on:

- The most relevant point  
- The clearest phrasing  
- The most useful example  

Avoid trying to cover everything.

---

## Common Signs of AI-Generated Writing

- Overly formal tone  
- Repetitive sentence structure  
- Generic filler language  
- Excessive enthusiasm  
- Over-structured formatting  
- Constant symmetry  
- Unnecessary summaries  
- Uniform certainty  
- Lack of voice or opinion  

---

## Editing Checklist

Use this when reviewing output:

1. Does this sound human or mechanical?  
2. Are there generic or canned phrases?  
3. Is the tone appropriate?  
4. Can abstract language be made concrete?  
5. Is sentence rhythm varied?  
6. Is anything over-explained?  
7. Does the text have a clear voice?  

---

## Summary

Human-like writing is:

- Intentional  
- Selective  
- Context-aware  
- Slightly imperfect  
- Clear and grounded  

Avoid optimizing for perfection. Optimize for believability.
